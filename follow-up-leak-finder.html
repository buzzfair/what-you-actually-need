Follow-Up Leak Finder

Find where interest is leaking before it becomes a decision.

Most solo operators do not lose business at the sales conversation. They lose it in the silence between the first signal of interest and the moment of decision. Someone visits your site, reads your content, attends a call — and then nothing happens. Not because they were not interested. Because the path forward was not clear enough, timely enough, or easy enough to act on.

The Funnel Leak Checklist

Answer yes or no to each item. The more "no" answers, the bigger the leak.

  • Do you have a clear, low-friction next step visible on your website or in your content?
  • When someone expresses interest (DM, inquiry, reply), do you have a standard response ready within 24 hours?
  • Do you follow up at least once after an inquiry if you do not hear back?
  • Do you have a way for interested people to hear from you regularly (email list, newsletter)?
  • After a sales conversation, do you send a follow-up email within 24–48 hours?
  • Do you have a sequence of two to three emails that go out after someone joins your list?
  • Do you track which leads are warm but have not yet moved forward?
  • Is there a clear "decide now" moment or buying window in your process?

Where follow-through usually breaks

  • The first follow-up never happens because it feels pushy
  • There is no sequence — only one-off emails that depend on memory
  • The "next step" is unclear, so interested people do nothing
  • Warm leads go cold because the gap between interest and the next touchpoint is too long
  • You assume silence means no, when it often means "I just need a nudge"

One sequence to install first

You do not need a complex funnel. Start with this:

1

Email 1 — same day or next day

Thank them for connecting. Restate what you do in one clear sentence. Offer one useful thing — a resource, a question, or an observation.

2

Email 2 — three days later

Share a relevant story, case example, or piece of thinking. No hard ask.

3

Email 3 — seven days later

A clear, direct invitation. What is the next step? Make it easy to take.

Three emails. Sent on a schedule. That closes more leaks than most elaborate funnels.
