Who this is for

  • You know (roughly) what you want built — a workflow, tool, sequence, system, or asset
  • You have been researching, planning, or attempting it for longer than the actual build would take
  • You are not blocked by strategy — you are blocked by execution
  • You want something working, not another framework to follow
  • You are ready to scope a specific deliverable and move

Who this is not for

  • People who are still figuring out what they want
  • People who want open-ended consulting or ongoing coaching
  • People expecting a giant custom software project for a sprint price
  • People who are not prepared to make fast decisions on scope and direction
If you are not yet sure what to build, the Diagnostic Strategy Session may be a better first step.

What is included

  • Scoping call (20–30 minutes) A short focused call to define the deliverable precisely before any work begins.
  • The build itself Designed for your tools, your workflow, and your situation — not a generic template.
  • One round of revisions Structured feedback incorporated after initial delivery.
  • Handoff document or walkthrough So you can use and maintain what was built without being dependent on anyone.
  • Follow-up window A window for questions after delivery — so you can get unstuck if something comes up in practice.

What is not included

  • Ongoing support or maintenance
  • Training your team or staff
  • Open-ended consulting outside the scoped deliverable
  • Rebuilds or pivots mid-sprint without a scope change discussion

How pricing works

Sprints start at $750. The actual price depends on scope, complexity, and the tools involved. Simple automations and single-sequence builds tend to fall in the $750–$1,000 range. More complex systems, multi-step workflows, or builds requiring platform integrations typically run $1,000–$1,500 or more. Scope is confirmed before work begins. You will not be surprised.
