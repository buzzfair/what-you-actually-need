**Purpose:** Guide the practitioner through the right next-step recommendation after each Diagnostic Strategy Session. This is a routing and communication tool, not a sales script.

## Section 2: Post-Session Decision Logic

Use the primary finding from the session to determine the recommended next step. One recommendation per client. Do not present multiple options simultaneously.

| Post-session finding | Recommended next step | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Visibility is the primary bottleneck | Visibility Planning Session | The Diagnostic Session surfaces visibility as the constraint; the Visibility Planning Session is built to address exactly that — it covers channel selection, content rhythm, and sustainable output without overlap with the broader diagnostic work |
| Implementation is the primary bottleneck | AI Build Sprint | The client has enough strategic clarity; what is missing is something built. The Build Sprint installs one functional system, sequence, or tool rather than adding more planning |
| Offer clarity needs deeper work | Free resource first (Offer Clarity path), then revisit in 3-4 weeks; second Diagnostic Session if layers are complex | Offer clarity is primarily a thinking and articulation process. It responds better to self-directed work with structured prompts than to another real-time session. A second session makes sense only if the first revealed that multiple interrelated constraints are present |
| Sales system needs structure | AI Build Sprint (follow-up sequence or CRM workflow build) | Most sales system bottlenecks have a structural cause — missing follow-up, no handoff protocol, no pipeline visibility. One installed system addresses this more directly than continued strategy conversation |
| Overwhelm | Free resource first (Noise Reduction Reset); revisit in 2-4 weeks | Overwhelm is a condition that responds poorly to additional investment or new commitments. The right move is reduction before re-engagement. Pushing forward with a paid next step when a client is overwhelmed tends to worsen the situation |

**Note on complexity:** When a session surfaces two or more interacting bottlenecks of roughly equal weight, a second Diagnostic Session is appropriate before recommending any paid implementation offer. Do not skip this step by picking the most expensive-looking recommendation.

## Section 5: Upsell Restraint Guidelines

These are operating principles, not rules designed to reduce revenue. They exist because pushing the wrong next step after a diagnostic session undermines the entire value proposition of the session itself.

**Do not pitch multiple offers at once.** The session produces a recommendation. Offer that recommendation. If there is a meaningful secondary option, note it briefly. Do not present a menu.

**Do not pressure if the client is not ready.** Overwhelm clients in particular are not good candidates for immediate upsell. A client who is too stretched to implement what they already have will not benefit from more. Respecting that is how you build a relationship worth returning to.

**Do not default to the AI Build Sprint if the diagnosis says otherwise.** The Build Sprint is a useful offer, but it is only right when implementation is the actual bottleneck. Selling a build engagement to someone whose problem is positioning or offer clarity wastes their money and creates a mismatch between what was delivered and what was needed.

**Trust the diagnosis.** The entire product is built around the idea that working on the right problem matters. The post-session recommendation should follow that same logic. If the session revealed offer clarity as the bottleneck, recommend the offer clarity path — not the next thing up the ladder.

**When you are genuinely unsure, say so.** It is acceptable to end a session by saying: "I want to think through what I heard before I make a recommendation. I will send you a summary within 24 hours." A delayed honest recommendation is more valuable than an immediate one that is not fully grounded.
